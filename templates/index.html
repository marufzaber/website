About Me

My name is Maruf Zaber. I am a Software Engineering graduate student at the University of California, Irvine. I currently live in Southern California, a land Chumash and Tongva people used to call home a couple of centuries ago. I was born in Mymensingh, a small town in Bangladesh many moons ago. I speak Bangla, English, a tiny bit of Hindi, and a tinier bit of Spanish.

I write, most of the time with code, sometimes with words. I think and wonder about optimization, scalability, software engineering principles, leadership, mindfulness, and inequality. In my past life, I was Von Neumann reincarnated in a stray cat, living in a backpacker hostel in Patagonia. You can write that in my obituary. Not that I care much at that point.

My Work

My expertise lies in Software Testing, Software Architecture, Information Retrieval, and Compiler. My biggest skill is learning new technologies and tools rapidly and get things done. I designed and implemented a scalable regression test selection tool for Java Unit tests from scratch. I call this tool as TLDR — Too Long Didn’t Regress! My other works involve test-suite parallelization, inter-project dependency management, and efficient code-clone detection.

I will be joining Google, a leading internet company in Q2, 2021. I’ll be joining their UFO Engineering team. Unfortunately, it’s not Unidentified Flying Object but fortunately, it’s United Fulfillment Optimization. I’ll be helping scale Google's phenomenal growth by optimizing their ginormous server fleet in near real-time. More about the team is in this video.

Previously, I had worked at BRAC University, New York University, Abu Dhabi, UC Irvine, and Google (again!).
